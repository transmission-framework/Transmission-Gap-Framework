Plain English — what does this mean?
The gems and jewellery sector generated 36.9% more nominal gross value added between 2021–22 and 2023–24, a period coinciding with significant export growth. However, real wages per registered factory worker declined by 5.9% after inflation adjustment. Labour's share of gross value added fell from 43.1% to 40.8% and has not returned to its pre-growth baseline. Workers captured only 34.3% of incremental value — an 8.8 percentage point shortfall. This pattern is consistent with domestic labour institutions — piece-rate employment structures, contractor-mediated employment, and incomplete Labour Code implementation — failing to transmit export-driven gains to workers.
Data: Annual Survey of Industries (ASI), National Statistical Office — Table 1 and Table 3, NIC 2008. Real wages deflated by CPI-IW, Labour Bureau, Ministry of Labour and Employment. Methodology fully documented and replicable. Coverage: registered factory sector only (~274,000 registered workers in gems and jewellery; estimated 5 million total including informal workforce). Export growth context noted as background; domestic labour institution performance is the subject of measurement. Last updated: ASI 2023–24. Next update: ASI 2024–25, expected late 2026.
